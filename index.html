Seven Days in Kazakhstan
Almaty & the Tian Shan

A week split between the cosmopolitan culture of Almaty and the alpine lakes, sandstone canyons, and Kazakh village hospitality of the surrounding Tian Shan. Mid-range with selective splurges, moderate-to-high fitness, rental car for the road days.

Day 1
Sat · Aug 1
Almaty
Arrival Easy day

Arrive Almaty — soft landing

Overnight AirAsia X from KUL lands early morning. Drop bags, grab a long breakfast, and pace the day around a cable-car sunset rather than hard sightseeing.

  • ~05:00Land at ALA. Visa-free for Singapore passports up to 30 days. Grab tenge from an ATM in arrivals; buy a Beeline or Kcell SIM at the kiosk.
  • 06:30Taxi to hotel (Yandex Go works like Uber here, ~3,500–5,000 ₸). Early check-in if available; otherwise leave bags.
  • 10:00Breakfast at Daredzhani (Georgian, near Panfilov) — khachapuri and strong coffee.
  • 11:30Panfilov Park & Zenkov Cathedral — wooden Russian Orthodox cathedral built without a single nail. Memorial of Glory next door.
  • 13:30Green Bazaar (Zelyony Bazar) — horse meat, kurt, dried fruit, samsas. Snack-graze rather than sit-down lunch.
  • 15:00Hotel — nap. You've been awake 24+ hours. Take it.
  • 18:30Kok-Tobe cable car from Dostyk Ave for sunset over the city and Tian Shan ridge.
  • 20:30Dinner at Kazakh Auyl or Navat — beshbarmak, manti, kazy, and a shot of kumys for ceremony's sake.
Lodging
Almaty centre — Rixos Almaty (splurge), Dostyk Hotel (mid), or Renion Park near Panfilov.
Cost-value upgrade
Pay a bit more to be walking distance to Panfilov / Arbat. Halves your taxi load all week.
Day 2
Sun · Aug 2
Almaty
Culture Cuisine

Almaty — culture, history, the long lunch

A full city day. Soviet-era civic architecture, the Kazakh national story at the museum, then a steam at the Arasan baths and dinner at the city's best Kazakh table.

  • 09:00Coffee + walk down Arbat (Zhibek Zholy pedestrian street).
  • 10:00Central State Museum of Kazakhstan — golden Saka warrior, nomadic material culture, Soviet years to independence (~2 hrs).
  • 12:30Republic Square & Independence Monument; loop past the brutalist Hotel Kazakhstan.
  • 13:30Lunch at Tubeteika (modern Central Asian) or Lanzhou (Dungan/Uyghur lagman) — both legendary, both cheap.
  • 15:30Arasan Baths — Soviet-era bath complex with Russian, Finnish, and Turkish rooms. Rent a felt hat; it's not optional.
  • 18:00Stroll Dostyk Ave; aperitif at the rooftop of Plaza or Esentai.
  • 20:00Dinner at Auyl — the city's flagship Kazakh restaurant in a yurt-style space; book ahead. The horse-meat tasting is the move.
Splurge worth it
Auyl. Best representation of Kazakh cuisine in the country.
If wet weather
Swap Republic Sq. for the Museum of Folk Musical Instruments — small, charming, in Panfilov Park.
Day 3
Mon · Aug 3
Big Almaty Lake
Hike Alpine

Big Almaty Lake — first taste of the Tian Shan

An accessible, knockout day hike from town. Glacial-blue lake at 2,500 m ringed by 4,000 m peaks. Pick up the rental car late afternoon for tomorrow's drive.

  • 07:30Hotel breakfast; pack layers, water (2 L pp), sunscreen, snacks.
  • 08:30Yandex taxi or pre-arranged driver to the BAL viewpoint trailhead at the dam (~1 hr, ~6,000 ₸ one way; arrange return). Note: a checkpoint requires passport — bring it.
  • 10:00Hike: walk the lake's east shore to the second viewpoint (~3–4 hrs round-trip, gentle). Strong hikers can continue toward the cosmostation at 3,300 m for a longer day.
  • 14:30Late lunch back in town — Salam Bro for shashlik, or Bao for Vietnamese.
  • 16:30Pick up rental car (Avis / Sixt / local Naprokat). 4×4 strongly recommended for the Saty road. Confirm IDP, full tank, and that the spare is real.
  • 19:30Dinner at Brioche (modern, lighter) or Mamasita if you want a break from steppe food before two car days.
Hike stats
~8–12 km · 200–600 m elevation depending on turnaround · 3–6 hrs.
Altitude note
Lake sits at 2,500 m. Most people are fine; go slow if you feel headachy.
Day 4
Tue · Aug 4
Almaty → Charyn → Saty
Canyon Road day

Charyn Canyon & the road to Saty village

Big driving day, but the kind you book the trip for: Almaty's apple orchards giving way to dry steppe, then Charyn's red-rock spires, then a high pass into the Kolsai valley.

  • 07:00Depart Almaty east on the A351. Stop at a roadside samsa stand in Chilik for breakfast.
  • 10:30Charyn Canyon — Valley of Castles. Park at the rim, hike down (~1 km, ~150 m descent) into the canyon floor, walk through the spires to the Charyn River. Allow 2.5–3 hrs total. Hot & exposed — start early, hat & water non-negotiable.
  • 13:30Lunch at the canyon yurt café (lagman, plov, tea) before pushing on.
  • 15:00Drive to Saty via Kegen — ~140 km, ~2.5 hrs. Last stretch is rough gravel; take it slow. Pin offline maps before you lose signal.
  • 18:00Check in at a Saty guesthouse (Zhanyl, Asyl, or Zhibek Zholy) — family-run homes with private rooms, banya, home-cooked dinner included.
  • 20:00Dinner with the host family — typically lagman or beshbarmak, fresh bread, jam, dairy. Early to bed.
Driving
~340 km · 5–6 hrs total moving time. Police speed checks are common — keep to the limits.
Cash
Saty has no ATMs. Withdraw enough tenge in Almaty for guesthouses, park fees, fuel detours.
Day 5
Wed · Aug 5
Kolsai & Kaindy
Big hike Lakes

Kaindy's drowned forest + the Kolsai traverse

The marquee day. Two of the most photographed places in Kazakhstan, and a real hike between them. Aim Kaindy first while it's quiet, then Kolsai for the long afternoon.

  • 07:30Quick breakfast at the guesthouse — eggs, kasha, bread, jam. Pack lunch from the kitchen.
  • 08:30Drive to Kaindy Lake (~30 min) — last 4 km is rough; either drive your 4×4 or hire a UAZ shuttle from the gate (~2,500 ₸ pp). Walk the shoreline; the submerged Tien Shan spruce trunks are what you came for. ~1.5 hrs.
  • 11:30Drive to Lower Kolsai (~45 min). Pay the national-park fee at the gate.
  • 12:30Hike Lower → Middle Kolsai. ~8 km one way, ~600 m up through Tien Shan spruce forest, finishing at a turquoise lake at 2,250 m. Allow 5–6 hrs round trip with photos and lunch lakeside.
  • 19:00Back to Saty. Banya at the guesthouse, then dinner.
Hike stats
~16 km round trip · ~600 m gain · 5–6 hrs. Trail is well-trodden but rooty & muddy in spots.
If you want more
Camp by Middle Kolsai and continue to Upper Kolsai (third lake) the next morning. Skip Day 6 leisurely return for an extra night in the mountains.
Day 6
Thu · Aug 6
Saty → Issyk → Almaty
Drive Last night

The long way back via Issyk Lake

A scenic return route over the Asy Plateau (weather permitting) or the standard road via Chilik. End the trip with a celebratory dinner in Almaty.

  • 08:00Goodbye breakfast at the guesthouse, settle up in cash.
  • 09:00Drive toward Almaty. Optional detour: Issyk Lake (Esik) — alpine lake re-formed after the 1963 mudflow, easy lakeshore stroll, decent café (~1 hr off the route).
  • 14:00Arrive Almaty; return rental car at the agency. Re-check into a downtown hotel.
  • 15:30Free afternoon: Esentai Mall for souvenirs (felt slippers, Almaty apples are in season, cognac), or a coffee at Wood Coffee on Tulebaeva.
  • 19:30Final dinner — splurge. Zheti Kazyna for refined Kazakh tasting, or Sandyq in the foothills for a yurt-style farewell.
Driving
~290 km · 4.5 hrs without detour, +1 hr for Issyk.
Pre-flight admin
Repack so your hand luggage holds anything fragile/valuable; AirAsia X has strict cabin weights.

Departure

Fly home

Morning flight to KUL, connect to SIN. ALA airport is 25 minutes from downtown but plan for 60 with traffic.

  • 06:30Hotel breakfast / pack-out.
  • 07:30Yandex to ALA. Be at the airport 3 hrs before departure for an international AirAsia X flight.
  • 10:30ALA → KUL. Connect at KLIA2.
  • laterKUL → SIN. Home.
Souvenirs
Apple cognac, kurt (the dried curd kind), felt-work, dombra strings if you've made a friend.
Don't bring back

Visa & entry

Singapore passportVisa-free for stays up to 30 days.
Passport validity6 months beyond entry, 1 blank page.
Onward ticketSometimes asked for at check-in; have your AirAsia X return printed.
RegistrationHotels register you automatically. Guesthouses in Saty usually do too — confirm.

Money & SIM

CurrencyKazakhstani tenge (₸). Roughly 1 SGD ≈ 350–380 ₸.
CardsWidely accepted in Almaty (Visa/MC). Cash-only outside the city — withdraw before driving out.
SIMBeeline or Kcell at ALA arrivals. ~5,000 ₸ for a tourist data pack. Coverage drops in the Kolsai/Saty area.
AppsYandex Go (taxis), 2GIS (offline maps for Almaty & oblasts), Google Translate Russian/Kazakh offline pack.

Driving

SideRight-hand drive. International Driving Permit required.
VehicleCompact SUV / 4×4 strongly recommended for the Saty road and Kaindy track. Confirm spare, jack, and warning triangle.
FuelAI-92 / AI-95 widely available; top up before leaving Almaty and again at Chilik or Kegen.
PoliceSpeed checks frequent on the A351. Stick to limits, keep documents handy.
Cost~SGD 90–140/day for a 4×4 from a major agency, less from local outfits.
